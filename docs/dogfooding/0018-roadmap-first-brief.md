# Dogfooding finding 0018 — The brief reports findings; the reader wants the roadmap first

- **Date:** 2026-07-11
- **Session:** dogfooding — *writing-assistant*, first morning read of the redesigned
  (Epic 8-era) brief: the 2026-07-10 forced re-run's `MORNING.md`, generated by a build
  carrying stories 8.1/8.2 (status line, First action, fold) but predating 8.4's ratio +
  remaining-criteria rendering.
- **Command:** none — the subject is the brief's composition priorities.
- **Classification:** output usability / composition. Successor to
  [0016](0016-morning-brief-usability.md): the 30-second mechanics landed (status line, one
  first action, fold), but the *content* the reader values most is still not what leads.
- **Status:** documented; verbatim user feedback, recorded for triage against what Epic 8
  already shipped.

## The feedback (user's own framing)

> The highest-value output for me is a clear, at-a-glance roadmap. That is the primary
> reason I started building Nightwatch. I expected the morning brief to answer these three
> questions immediately:
>
> 1. What is the current, agreed-upon final goal?
> 2. What are the concrete remaining steps to reach that goal?
> 3. Where am I currently on that path?
>
> Instead, the brief focuses on findings and status reports, but I still have to reconstruct
> the overall objective and remaining work myself. I would like the roadmap to be the first
> thing I see every morning.

## Analysis against what already shipped

The brief-composition spec (FR55) leads with *triage* — "is anything on fire, what do I do
first" — and places the release position ("Where you stand") third, after the action lists.
The user's mental model leads with *orientation*: goal → remaining steps → position, with
findings as servants of that path, not the headline.

Partial overlaps with work already merged, and the genuine gaps:

- **Q1 (final goal):** the target string renders in "Where you stand" — but as a clause
  inside a progress line, not as a stated goal. Nothing renders `STATE.md`'s
  `definition_of_done` as the goal contract it is.
- **Q2 (remaining steps):** Story 8.4 (merged after the run under review) adds the titles of
  remaining definition-of-done items and the done/total ratio to "Where you stand" — a
  meaningful part of the answer. But `RELEASE.md`'s "Next actions (top 3)" — the closest
  thing to "concrete remaining steps" — still never reaches the brief.
- **Q3 (where am I):** the ratio (8.4) answers this numerically; the user asks for it
  *positionally* — a path with a marker on it, not a percentage.
- **Ordering:** all of this sits *below* the findings-derived action lists. The user asks for
  the inversion: roadmap first, then tonight's findings as movements along it.

## What this suggests (observations, not yet design)

- Consider a **Roadmap block at the very top** (or immediately after the status line): the
  declared target, the definition-of-done as an ordered checklist with done/remaining marks,
  and the tracker's top next actions — i.e., render the *tracker's* spine in the brief,
  rather than only findings.
- Tonight's findings could be *attached to* roadmap steps where they cross-reference
  (release-progress already links findings to DoD items) — findings as progress/obstacles on
  the path, not a parallel taxonomy.
- The tension to resolve at spec time: FR55's triage-first contract ("is anything on fire")
  vs. roadmap-first. A possible synthesis: status line stays first (fire check is one line),
  roadmap second, first-action third — the first action often *is* roadmap step 1.
